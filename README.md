# SG Math Whiz

A Wordle-inspired daily math challenge for adults — one PSLE-style question per day, submitted once, ranked by speed. Built as a prototype to test whether Singapore's reputation for rigorous primary school mathematics could be the basis of a fun, brain-training habit for grown-ups.

## Why it's no longer live

The concept worked. The content pipeline didn't.

Sourcing one quality question per day — clear, unambiguous, solvable without a diagram that breaks on mobile — turned out to be the hard part. Attempts to automate it ran into a two-sided problem: OCR-based extraction from scanned exam papers was accurate enough to be useful but expensive enough to be unsustainable at volume, and even with good extraction, programmatic filtering by difficulty and automatic answer verification produced too many false positives. Diagram-heavy questions (a staple of PSLE math) were particularly resistant to automation — reliably extracting both the image and the correct answer in a form suitable for a web app proved harder than expected.

Manual curation was the only approach that reliably produced good questions, and that wasn't a pipeline — it was a part-time job.

Taken offline May 2026.
